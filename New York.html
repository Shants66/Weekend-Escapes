New York, New York - the city that never sleeps

Day One

Brunch

Brunch is the most important meal of the week in New York! Here are some of our favourites:

  • Sadelle's (Soho)

    • - you must try the Sadelle's Tower and the french toast

  • Upland (Flatiron)

    • - great pizzas, pasta and lots more

  • Santina (Meatpacking)

    • - good food and great cocktails

The High Line

Less well-known amongst tourists, this elevated railway track has been transformed into a public park with stunning greenery, featuring seasonal food vendors and budding artists. When the sun's out, there's nothing quite like taking a walk on the High Line. Join the High Line at 34th Sreet and 11th Avenue, and walk down to Chelsea Market; an urban food court with lots of cool shops.

Brooklyn Bridge

A New York icon, the Brooklyn Bridge was the world’s first steel suspension bridge, and at 1596ft, the longest when it opened in 1883. Its suspended bicycle/pedestrian walkway delivers soul-stirring views of Manhattan, the East River and the Brooklyn waterfront. If you walk from Manhattan over to Brooklyn, you should also check out Brooklyn Bridge Park and DUMBO.

Day Two

Central Park

Take an early-morning stroll through this 843-acre-sized patch of greenery. The park is a great spot to feel submerged in nature, far from the bustle of the big city.

Museums

New York is the home to some of the best museums in the world. Conveniently, the Guggenheim, MOMA and The Met are a short stroll away from Central Park.

Shopping

5th Avenue and Madison Avenue are great places to check out world-renowned designers, with SoHo and Chelsea also offering a great array of boutiques to explore.

Times Square & Broadway

It wouldn’t be a trip to New York without a stop by Broadway. After grabbing dinner in Hell's Kitchen (9th Avenue), swing by Times Square and try and catch a show on Broadway.
